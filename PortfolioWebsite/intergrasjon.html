Integration Project (aka. Mini Bachelor)

PROG2052

Subject Description

In this course, students work on a group project where they combine and expand upon themes from various individual topics in their study program. Because of this, the course is often referred to as a "mini-bachelor," serving as a prequel to the bachelor's project. In their project, the students will integrate and work with themes such as mobile/web-applications, distributed/centralized solutions, graphics/game-programming, artificial intelligence, database, cloud technologies, networking or virtualization.

Through the project work, the students will work with agile development processes, project planning, requirements specification, design, implementation, testing, sustainability, artificial intelligence, information security and documentation.

Our project

In this project, we brainstormed several ideas but ultimately decided on one called FridgeIO. The concept was a website where you, along with your family or roommates, could add ingredients stored in the fridge and cabinets, track common items, and log expiration dates to reduce food waste. It also allows you to check what you already have at home, even while you're in the store. This was the foundation of the idea. On the right, you can see a screenshot of the application. We placed a strong emphasis on mobile users throughout the project, as we anticipated that most people would use the app either in the kitchen or while shopping, where laptops or desktop computers are less convenient.

The process of the project

In this project, we dedicated a significant amount of time to perfecting the database structure, as well as creating a user-friendly and functional wireframe. On the left, you can see the ERD (Entity-Relationship Diagram) for the project. Since the data and functionality were relatively simple, the ERD turned out to be straightforward and easy to work with. The wireframe reflected the design we aimed to implement, prioritizing usability. However, due to time constraints, limited experience at the time, and challenges in integrating some features, we were unable to fully achieve our design goals.
